Leading a Cross-Channel Design System from Scratch

Built and led the development of a scalable multi-channel design system from scratch, establishing a single source of truth that improved visual consistency, accelerated delivery, and enabled long-term product growth.

Role
Lead Designer

Background

As the team transitioned from fully outsourced design to a fully in-house model, we inherited products without a structured design system. Design assets were previously managed across vendor files with no centralised system for internal teams to reference or maintain.

As ownership shifted internally, it became essential to establish a scalable and maintainable foundation to support ongoing product development across multiple channels.

Problem

There was no single source of truth for design assets, components, or usage guidelines. Designers had to manually trace previous files to locate references or recreate components from scratch, often resulting in inconsistencies across products.

This lack of standardisation led to slower design turnaround times, duplicated effort, and fragmented user experiences across different product channels.

Challenge

I led the initiative to build the design system from the ground up while continuing to support active product development. This required defining the system's structure, establishing governance, and acting as the primary reviewer for new components and assets.

The challenge was not only to create the system itself, but also to drive alignment across designers and ensure adoption — balancing scalability, usability, and long-term maintainability across multiple product channels.

Opportunity

There was opportunity to establish a unified design system to serve as a single source of truth, improving consistency, scalability, and efficiency across products. A well-structured system would enable faster delivery, reduce duplicated work, and create a cohesive user experience across products.

Solution

I led the design and implementation of a scalable, multi-channel design system structured across foundational elements, reusable components, and channel-specific assets.

1

Established shared foundations

Core elements such as typography, colour, spacing, and iconography were centralised in a shared library, providing a consistent visual language across all products.

2

Structured channel-specific component libraries

Dedicated design system files were created for each product channel to house components, templates, and flows relevant to their needs, while still aligning with the shared foundation.

3

Defined system architecture and governance

Established naming conventions, categorisation structures, and contribution guidelines to ensure consistency and scalability, including clear criteria for introducing and reviewing new components to maintain system integrity.

4

Created documentation and usage guidelines

Guidelines were established for component usage, asset creation, and icon design, ensuring both existing and new designers could contribute consistently and confidently.

5

Led adoption and cross-team alignment

Facilitated discussions, reviews, and approvals to ensure alignment across teams and establish the design system as the trusted source of truth.

Redesigning In-App Feedback to Improve Net Promoter Score

Transformed fragmented, out-of-context feedback into actionable in-app insights — enabling better product prioritisation, strengthening user trust, and significantly increasing Net Promoter Score (NPS).

Role
UI/UX Designer

Background

Previously, users received an SMS with a link to a feedback form after completing certain actions in the app. While this approach generated responses, the feedback was often disconnected from the user's immediate experience, resulting in broad and low-quality insights that were difficult for teams to interpret and act on.

This project focused on redesigning the feedback experience to make it contextual, timely, and actionable.

Problem

The existing feedback system lacked contextual relevance, making it difficult to derive meaningful insights.

Surveys were not triggered in direct relation to specific user interactions, causing feedback to be untimely and overly general. As a result, teams struggled to identify friction points or link feedback to specific journeys.

This diluted the value of the data and limited the team's ability to confidently prioritise and improve key user flows.

Opportunity

Introduce contextual, in-app feedback at key interaction points to capture timely, relevant insights through guided inputs. This enables users to reflect accurately on their recent experience while generating both measurable and actionable feedback.

Solution

We redesigned the feedback system into a fully integrated, contextual in-app survey framework to capture more relevant and actionable insights.

1

In-app contextual surveys

Replaced generic SMS links with surveys embedded directly within the app, triggered immediately after five high-impact user actions to capture feedback at the most relevant moment.

2

Journey-based feedback triggers

Mapped each survey trigger to specific user journeys, allowing teams to clearly associate feedback with real user interactions and identify friction points more accurately.

3

Guided and actionable responses

Introduced quick-select scenario options tailored to each trigger, helping users clearly express their experience while retaining open-text fields for deeper qualitative insights.

This approach significantly improved feedback quality, clarity, and usability — making insights more actionable and easier for teams to analyse and prioritise.

Impact

The redesigned in-app feedback system significantly improved feedback quality and strengthened customer sentiment, turning feedback into a reliable driver of product improvements.

Most notably, the app's NPS increased from -38.5 to 32.1 within a year, reflecting meaningful experience improvements enabled by clearer, contextual insights.

By capturing feedback at critical interaction points, teams were able to identify friction faster and prioritise improvements with greater confidence. This strengthened cross-functional alignment and reinforced user trust by ensuring users felt heard at the moments that mattered most.

Pay-as-you-go (PAYG) Roaming UX Revamp

Redesigned the Pay-as-you-go roaming experience to improve clarity, align with user mental models, and reduce unintended usage of higher-cost roaming services.

Role
UI/UX Designer

Background

Pay-as-you-go (PAYG) roaming allows users to stay connected overseas without subscribing to a roaming plan, charging them only based on actual usage. This service is designed to provide flexibility for users who prefer not to commit to fixed roaming plans.

Problem

The existing PAYG roaming experience is confusing and misleading due to unclear hierarchy and interaction design.

Voice & SMS roaming is enabled by default, but the interface presents it as a subscription option. As a result, when users unsubscribe from PAYG Data roaming, they may mistakenly believe they have also unsubscribed from Voice & SMS roaming, prompting unnecessary actions to re-enable a service that was already active.

Additionally, PAYG roaming is given equal or higher visual hierarchy compared to roaming plans, despite being relevant to a smaller group of users. This creates confusion and leads some users to unintentionally rely on PAYG data roaming, which incurs higher usage-based charges compared to purchasing roaming plans.

Before

Opportunity

The PAYG roaming service can be repositioned to better align with users' mental models and product hierarchy.

Instead of presenting PAYG as a standalone primary section, it can be integrated within the add-ons or value-added services section, where users naturally expect optional services to appear. Clarifying the default status of Voice & SMS roaming, while presenting PAYG Data as an optional add-on, can reduce confusion and improve decision-making.

Solution

I redesigned the information architecture and interface to improve clarity, hierarchy, and usability of the PAYG roaming experience.

1

Repositioned PAYG to align with user expectations

Moved PAYG roaming under Active add-ons and reduced its visual prominence, aligning it with users' mental model of optional services while prioritising roaming plans as the primary option.

2

Clarified default Voice & SMS roaming

Presented Voice & SMS roaming as an active default service, removing the impression that users needed to subscribe to enable it.

3

Made PAYG Data roaming a clear optional add-on

Separated PAYG Data roaming from the default service, helping users better understand its purpose and avoid accidental activation.

4

Improved content structure and usability

Refined grouping, labels, and layout to reduce cognitive load and make roaming options easier to understand and navigate.

Impact

The redesign improved clarity among users around PAYG roaming.

Users can now clearly distinguish between default Voice & SMS roaming and the optional PAYG Data roaming add-on. Repositioning PAYG under add-ons also aligned the experience with user expectations, making the roaming section easier to understand and navigate.

This reduced the number of users mistakenly subscribing to PAYG Data roaming and helped guide users toward more suitable roaming plans, resulting in a clearer experience and more effective service adoption.

App Voucher Wallet

Designed a centralised Voucher Wallet to improve voucher discoverability, increase redemption, and strengthen the app as a key promotional and engagement channel.

Role
UI/UX Designer

Background

Vouchers were previously only accessible through the website, while the app primarily focused on account management features such as viewing plans, usage, and billing. This limited the app's role as a channel for promotional engagement and reward discovery.

Problem

The lack of voucher visibility within the app resulted in low voucher redemption rates and missed opportunities to engage users.

Challenge

Although vouchers could be introduced and managed within the app, they could only be applied on the website. The solution needed to create a seamless experience that allows users to discover and redeem vouchers in the app, while smoothly transitioning them to the website to complete usage.

Opportunity

Introducing a centralised Voucher Wallet within the app would provide a single, dedicated space for users to view, manage, and redeem vouchers across all their services. This would improve reward visibility, align with users' expectations of managing their account in one place, and strengthen the app's role as a key engagement channel.

Solution

I designed a centralised Voucher Wallet to improve voucher discoverability, clarity, and usability:

  • Created a dedicated Voucher Wallet section within the app for easy access
  • Enabled account holders to view and manage vouchers across all their services and lines
  • Designed clear voucher cards with key information, including value, expiry, and eligibility
  • Introduced sorting options, such as recently added and earliest expiry, to help users prioritise usage
  • Provided a seamless transition to the website for voucher application and completion
  • Designed empty states and filtering to improve clarity and usability

Impact

The Voucher Wallet made vouchers more visible, accessible, and easier to manage within the app. Users can now easily discover, track, and redeem vouchers in one place, improving convenience and overall satisfaction.

For the business, this increased voucher redemption, boosted app engagement, and enabled more effective promotional campaigns by surfacing relevant rewards directly within the app.
